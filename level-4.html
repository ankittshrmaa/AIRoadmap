LEVEL 4

Advanced AI Specialization

Dive deep into cutting-edge AI fields and develop expertise in your chosen specialization

6-8 months intensive
Goal: Become a domain expert in chosen AI specialization

By Now You Should:

  • Know ML & DL fundamentals (from previous levels)
  • Be comfortable building models in PyTorch
  • Understand CNNs, RNNs, LSTMs, and basic NLP/CV concepts
  • Have experience with at least 5 deep learning projects

Choose Your Specialization Track(s)

Pick 1 primary + optionally 1 secondary track to focus on:

1

NLP & LLMs

3-4 months focus

Chatbots, AI assistants, text summarization, question answering systems using large language models.

Core Skills

  • Tokenization, embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe, FastText)
  • Transformers: self-attention, encoder/decoder
  • HuggingFace Transformers library
  • Fine-tuning pre-trained LLMs
  • Prompt engineering & retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)

Projects

  • Domain-specific chatbot using GPT-style model
  • Document summarizer for long PDFs
  • Q&A bot with custom data (RAG + vector DB)
2

Computer Vision

3-4 months focus

Object detection, segmentation, image-to-image translation, and AI art generation.

Core Skills

  • YOLO, Faster R-CNN, Detectron2
  • U-Net, Mask R-CNN for segmentation
  • Image captioning models
  • Generative models: GANs, diffusion models
  • Advanced data augmentation

Projects

  • Real-time object detection with YOLOv8
  • AI art generator (Stable Diffusion fine-tuning)
  • Medical image segmentation for diagnostics
3

Generative AI

4 months focus

Text-to-image, text-to-video, and multi-input AI systems combining different modalities.

Core Skills

  • GANs, Variational Autoencoders (VAEs)
  • Diffusion models (DALL·E, Stable Diffusion)
  • CLIP (connecting text and images)
  • Multi-modal transformers (Flamingo, BLIP-2)
  • Text-to-video generation basics

Projects

  • Text-to-image generator using diffusion models
  • Multi-modal search engine
  • Automated meme generator using CLIP + GPT
4

Speech & Audio AI

3-4 months focus

Speech recognition, voice cloning, music generation, and audio processing systems.

Core Skills

  • MFCC, spectrograms and audio features
  • Speech-to-text (Whisper, DeepSpeech)
  • Text-to-speech (Tacotron 2, FastSpeech)
  • Voice cloning technology
  • Music generation (Jukebox, MusicLM)

Projects

  • Voice-controlled AI assistant
  • Podcast transcription + summarization system
  • AI music generator in specific styles
5

Domain-Specific AI

3-4 months focus

High-paying niche applications in healthcare, finance, legal, or other specialized domains.

Core Skills

  • Domain-specific data and regulations
  • Tailoring models to compliance needs
  • Domain-specific evaluation metrics
  • Interpretability & explainable AI
  • Integration with domain software

Projects

  • Financial risk predictor
  • Medical diagnosis assistant
  • Legal document analyzer

Essential Skills for All Tracks

  • Model deployment at scale: Docker + Kubernetes + cloud AI services
  • MLOps for deep learning: MLflow, Weights & Biases integration
  • Distributed training: Scaling across multiple GPUs
  • API productization: Building production-ready AI services
  • Experiment tracking: Ensuring reproducibility

Career Strategy Recommendation

For maximum job security and opportunities in 2024-2025:

  • Primary Track: NLP/LLMs (most in-demand currently)
  • Secondary Track: Domain-Specific AI (makes you the rare expert in regulated industries like healthcare/finance)

Stage 4 Capstone Project

Build a portfolio-defining, end-to-end specialized AI system that could be shown to employers or investors.

Example: AI-Powered Legal Assistant

  • Uses RAG to answer legal questions from case files
  • Fine-tuned LLM for legal terminology
  • Deployed as web app with authentication
  • Logs interactions for continuous improvement

Deliverables

  • Production-ready code + Dockerfile
  • Dataset preprocessing scripts
  • README with architecture diagram
  • Demo video walkthrough
  • Performance benchmarks

Stage 4 Exit Criteria

You're ready for Stage 5 (Real-World AI Engineering) when you can:

  • Fine-tune and deploy models in your chosen specialization
  • Explain architecture trade-offs to non-technical stakeholders
  • Optimize inference for speed and cost efficiency
  • Demonstrate 3 advanced projects + 1 capstone in your portfolio
  • Integrate AI models with production systems
